\begin{abstract}
面向金融风控、反欺诈、反洗钱与交易监测等场景，异常检测系统既要识别少量高风险样本，又要在大规模数据条件下满足吞吐率和时延要求。随着交易规模持续增长，系统瓶颈已不再主要表现为单一检测模型能力不足，而更多集中在异常评分计算复杂、候选召回访问开销大、CPU 与 GPU 之间数据交换频繁以及多 GPU 扩展效率受限等环节。传统 CPU 方案难以同时兼顾大规模处理能力与近实时响应；如果只对个别算子或检索模块做局部加速，候选召回、异常评分和并行扩展之间的链路开销仍会限制系统整体性能。

针对上述问题，本文围绕金融大规模数据异常检测任务，设计并实现 PyTOD--FlashANNS 协同异常检测系统 FlashTOD，其中 Flash 取自面向大规模外存近似最近邻检索的 FlashANNS 候选召回系统，TOD 对应基于张量操作的 PyTOD 异常评分框架所沿用的 TOD 路线。FlashTOD 先在大规模数据中筛出候选集合，再在候选范围内完成张量化异常评分，并输出异常分数与前 $k$ 个结果（Top-$k$）风险对象。该系统重点在于组织候选召回与异常评分之间的数据流和执行顺序，而不是提出新的异常检测理论模型。

在单 GPU 与多 GPU 场景下，本文分别对 FlashTOD 的数据路径和扩展路径进行工程化组织。单卡链路在 FlashANNS 异步 I/O--计算流水线基础上，使查询特征、候选结果和评分中间张量尽可能在 GPU 侧完成组织与传递，仅在必要边界通过页锁定内存（pinned memory）与主机交互；同时引入 RAPIDS GPU 数据处理生态和 FAISS 的 GPU 检索实现（FAISS-GPU）重构特征处理、表格型中间结果和检索后端。多卡链路优先采用数据并行与索引复制式 replicated 架构，使每张 GPU 独立完成本地候选召回与异常评分；当跨卡归并不可避免时，借助 NVIDIA 集合通信库（NCCL）实现设备间通信，并结合拓扑感知的 I/O 通道（I/O lane）绑定与准入控制减轻共享路径竞争。

实验部分使用约 1M 规模的真实或半真实金融数据集验证系统有效性，并基于高保真合成数据构建 10M、50M 与 100M 规模实验环境。在本文设定的数据规模、硬件平台和候选预算条件下，完整融合方案的精确率—召回率曲线下面积（PR-AUC）为 0.906，接近 PyTOD 全量评分的 0.932；同时每秒查询数（QPS）由全量评分的 2700 提升至 4280。单卡递进式消融显示，完整优化方案相较原始分段链路将 QPS 从 3180 提升至 4550，并将第 99 百分位延迟（p99 延迟）从 67.0 ms 降至 45.5 ms；在 replicated 路径下，1/2/4 GPU 的 QPS 分别为 4550、7780 和 13620。总体而言，本文为高吞吐、近实时金融异常检测系统提供了一条以系统设计、链路组织和 GPU 并行实现为核心的工程实现路径。
\end{abstract}

\begin{abstract*}
In financial risk control, fraud detection, anti-money laundering, and transaction monitoring, outlier detection systems must identify a small number of high-risk samples while still meeting strict throughput and latency requirements at scale. As transaction volume grows, the main bottleneck is no longer limited to the accuracy of an individual model. It increasingly comes from expensive anomaly scoring, costly candidate retrieval, frequent CPU--GPU data movement, and limited multi-GPU scalability. Traditional CPU-based solutions struggle to balance large-scale processing with near-real-time response, while isolated acceleration of individual operators or retrieval modules cannot remove the overhead across the full pipeline.

To address these issues, this thesis designs and implements \emph{FlashTOD}, a collaborative outlier detection system that combines PyTOD and FlashANNS for large-scale financial data. The name combines the FlashANNS candidate retrieval front end for out-of-core approximate nearest neighbor search with the TOD-oriented PyTOD anomaly scoring framework based on tensor operations. FlashTOD first narrows the search space through candidate retrieval, then performs tensorized anomaly scoring on the candidate set, and finally outputs anomaly scores and Top-$k$ risky objects. Its focus is the organization of data flow and execution order between candidate retrieval and anomaly scoring, rather than a new theoretical model for outlier detection.

For both single-GPU and multi-GPU settings, this thesis organizes the data path and scaling path of FlashTOD from an engineering perspective. In the single-GPU path, the system builds on the asynchronous I/O--compute pipeline of FlashANNS, keeps query features, candidate results, and intermediate scoring tensors on the GPU whenever possible, and limits host interaction to necessary boundaries through pinned memory. RAPIDS is introduced as a GPU data-processing ecosystem, while FAISS-GPU serves as the GPU retrieval implementation of FAISS to support feature processing, tabular intermediate organization, and the retrieval backend. In the multi-GPU path, the system primarily adopts data parallelism and an index-replicated architecture, allowing each GPU to complete local candidate retrieval and anomaly scoring independently. When cross-GPU merging is unavoidable, NCCL is used for inter-device communication, and topology-aware I/O lane binding with admission control is applied to reduce shared-path contention.

Experiments are conducted on real or semi-real financial datasets at around 1M scale and high-fidelity synthetic datasets at 10M, 50M, and 100M scales. Under the evaluated data scales, hardware platform, and candidate budgets, the full fusion scheme achieves a PR-AUC of 0.906, close to the 0.932 of the PyTOD full-scoring baseline, while increasing throughput from 2700 QPS to 4280 QPS. The progressive single-GPU ablation further increases QPS from 3180 to 4550 and reduces p99 latency from 67.0 ms to 45.5 ms, while the replicated path reaches 4550, 7780, and 13620 QPS on 1, 2, and 4 GPUs, respectively. Overall, FlashTOD provides an engineering path for high-throughput and near-real-time financial outlier detection through system design, pipeline organization, and GPU-parallel implementation.
\end{abstract*}
